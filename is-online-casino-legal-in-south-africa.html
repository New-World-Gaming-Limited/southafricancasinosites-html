Is Online Casino Gambling Legal in South Africa?

Online casino and interactive gambling is not currently lawful under South African law. This page explains the legal position, the distinction from sports betting, and the lawful alternatives.

Written by Thabo Mokoena Editor Last reviewed: March 2026 Fact-checked by Lerato Dlamini
Legal position: Online casino gambling is NOT lawful in South Africa. The National Gambling Act 7 of 2004 prohibits interactive gambling, which covers online casino games including slots, roulette and blackjack played over the internet. Why online casino is prohibited

The National Gambling Act defines "interactive gambling" as participating in a game of chance through an electronic communication and prohibits this activity unless specifically licensed. No licences for online casino operation have been issued in South Africa under this framework. South African Parliament considered interactive gambling regulation but no amendment has been enacted as of March 2026.

The lawful distinction: sports betting vs casino

The National Gambling Act treats sports betting and casino gambling as legally distinct. Sports betting (including horse racing) is permitted through the provincial licensing system and regulated by provincial gambling boards. Online casino games are prohibited regardless of where the platform is hosted. This is why this site covers licensed sports betting but does not link to any online casino operators.
