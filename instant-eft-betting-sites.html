How Instant EFT works

Instant EFT uses a secure redirect from the bookmaker's cashier to a payment processor, which then connects to your internet banking for authentication. You log in to your internet banking (you are on your bank's own secure page), authorise the payment, and the funds reach the bookmaker in real time.

Unlike a standard EFT which can take 1 to 3 hours to clear, an instant EFT is confirmed immediately. The bookmaker's system receives an instant notification and credits your account within seconds of authentication.

Common issues and how to avoid them

The most common reason Instant EFT deposits fail is a session timeout. If you take too long between initiating the payment on the bookmaker's site and completing the bank authentication, the session can expire. Start the process only when you have a few minutes of uninterrupted time. Use a stable internet connection, as dropped data can interrupt the bank authentication step.

Frequently asked questions

Instant EFT is an electronic funds transfer processed in real time. Rather than waiting hours for a standard EFT, the payment goes from your bank to the bookmaker's account immediately. You authenticate through your internet banking and the bookmaker receives funds without delay.

Most licensed South African bookmakers support Instant EFT, either through Ozow or their own banking integration. Betway, Hollywoodbets, Easybet, 10bet, Gbets and most others listed on this site support it.

Ozow is a specific service that facilitates instant EFT. Other providers such as Payfast and PayU also process instant EFT. When a bookmaker lists Instant EFT, it may be powered by one of these providers. Ozow is the most widely recognised brand in this category at South African betting sites.
