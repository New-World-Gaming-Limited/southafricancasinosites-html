Gambling Tax in South Africa 2026

Plain-language guide to how SARS treats betting and gambling winnings. When winnings are taxable, what recreational bettors need to know, and when to seek professional advice.

Written by Thabo Mokoena Editor Last reviewed: March 2026 Fact-checked by Lerato Dlamini
Important: This page provides general information only. It is not tax advice. South African tax law can be complex and your personal circumstances affect how SARS may treat your gambling activity. Always consult a qualified tax professional for personalised advice.

The general position: recreational betting

For most South African bettors who place occasional wagers as a hobby or recreation, betting winnings are not subject to income tax. SARS does not generally treat casual gambling as a trade, and winnings from a hobby activity are not ordinarily taxable under the Income Tax Act 58 of 1962.

When winnings could become taxable

If your betting activity is regular, systematic and commercial in nature - more closely resembling a business than a hobby - SARS may characterise it as a trade. In that case, net profits could be included in your taxable income. Factors SARS might consider include: frequency of betting, bet sizes, whether you bet using a system, and whether you derive a significant portion of your income from betting.

Levy on gambling industry (not bettors)

The South African government levies taxes on licensed gambling operators, not directly on bettors. Bookmakers pay various levies to their licensing provincial board and contribute to the National Responsible Gambling Programme fund. These costs are absorbed by operators as part of their licensing obligations.

Frequently asked questions

For most recreational bettors, betting winnings are not subject to income tax in South Africa. Gambling is not generally considered a trade, so winnings are not treated as income. However, if you bet very frequently, at high volume, or professionally, SARS may characterise your activity as a business and tax your net winnings as income. Consult a qualified tax professional if you are unsure.

Withholding tax on gambling winnings has been proposed and discussed in South African tax circles, but as of March 2026 no withholding tax specifically on gambling winnings has been enacted. The tax position may change - check SARS or a tax professional for the latest.

There is no specific gambling winnings declaration form in South Africa. If SARS considers your gambling activity to constitute a trade, any net profit would need to be included in your income tax return. Most casual bettors are not affected by this. Seek professional advice if your betting activity is significant.

You cannot deduct casual gambling losses from your personal income tax in South Africa. If SARS treats your gambling as a trade (rare), losses from that trade could potentially be offset against income from the same trade, subject to specific provisions.

SASSA (South African Social Security Agency) recommends that social grant recipients do not use grant funds for gambling. There is no specific tax provision related to SASSA grants and gambling, but it is a welfare concern that the National Responsible Gambling Programme addresses.
