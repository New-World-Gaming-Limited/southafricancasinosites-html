Betting payment methods in South Africa

A complete guide to deposit and withdrawal options at licensed South African bookmakers, including Capitec Pay, Ozow, Instant EFT, cards, bank transfer, vouchers and Skrill.

Written by Naledi Khumalo Reviewer Last reviewed: March 2026 Fact-checked by Thabo Mokoena

In South Africa, payment method choice is often a trust signal as much as a convenience factor. Local banking methods such as Capitec Pay and Ozow are familiar, fast and fee-free at most bookmakers. This hub guides you to the right method based on how you actually bank and what matters to you in terms of speed, control and cost.

Capitec Pay

Capitec Pay connects directly through the Capitec banking app. It is one of the most popular deposit methods among South African bettors because it is fast, free at most bookmakers, and does not require card details.

Deposit: Instant Withdrawal: 24-48 hours (via bank transfer) Fees: Free at most bookmakers Min: R10

Supported at: Easybet, Betway, Hollywoodbets, Supabets, YesPlay

Choosing the right payment method

Speed first

If speed is the priority, choose Capitec Pay, Ozow or Instant EFT for deposits. All three process in real time. Withdrawals at South African bookmakers universally go through bank transfer, typically in 24 to 72 hours depending on the operator.

Spending control

If you prefer prepaid spending control, 1Voucher lets you set a budget by purchasing a specific-value voucher. You cannot spend more than the voucher amount. The trade-off is that withdrawals cannot go through this route.

Familiar banking

If you prefer a traditional route, bank transfer works at every licensed operator and handles larger amounts well. Deposits may take 1 to 2 hours if sent as a manual EFT, while instant EFT is processed in real time.

E-wallet option

Skrill provides a layer of separation between your bank and the bookmaker, which some users prefer for privacy or account management. The trade-off is potential fees on deposits and withdrawals, especially when currency conversion is involved.
