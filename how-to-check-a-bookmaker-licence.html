How to Check a Bookmaker's South African Licence

A step-by-step guide to verifying that a South African bookmaker is properly licensed. Protect yourself by checking before you deposit.

Written by Thabo Mokoena Editor Last reviewed: March 2026 Fact-checked by Lerato Dlamini
Why this matters: Only bookmakers licensed by a South African provincial gambling board are lawfully operating in South Africa. A licence means the operator is subject to regulation, must protect customer funds, must provide responsible gambling tools, and is answerable to a local authority if things go wrong.

Step-by-step licence check

  1. Scroll to the footer of the bookmaker's website. Licensed SA bookmakers are required to display their licence information.
  2. Look for the licence holder name (the registered legal entity), the licence number, and the name of the issuing provincial gambling board.
  3. Note the board name - it will be one of the nine South African provincial gambling boards (e.g. Gauteng Gambling Board, Western Cape Gambling and Racing Board).
  4. Visit the board's website and use their public licence register or search function to confirm the operator name appears on the current list.
  5. If the licence is listed and current, you can be confident you are dealing with a regulated operator.

What to do if a bookmaker cannot provide licence details

If a bookmaker's website does not display any licence information, do not deposit. You can also contact the bookmaker's customer support and ask directly for their South African licence number and the name of the issuing board. A legitimate operator will answer this question immediately.
