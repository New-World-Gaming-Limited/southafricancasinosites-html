Offshore vs Licensed Betting: Why It Matters

Over 2,000 unlicensed operators target South African players. R50 billion leaves the economy annually. Here is why using an offshore site puts your money at risk.

What Happens When Things Go Wrong

With a licensed bookmaker, you have a clear path to resolution: operator support, provincial gambling board, National Gambling Board, Consumer Protection Act, Small Claims Court. With an offshore operator, you have none of these options.

  • Withheld winnings: An offshore operator can refuse to pay with zero consequences. No SA court has jurisdiction over a Curacao-registered company.
  • Account closure: Your account can be closed and funds seized without explanation. There is no regulator to appeal to.
  • Identity theft: Without FICA-grade data protection, your personal documents may be sold or misused.
  • Bank confiscation: The South African Reserve Bank can seize incoming transfers from offshore gambling operators under exchange control regulations.

Why SA Has Not Blocked Offshore Sites

Unlike the UK (which has blocked 375+ gambling sites since 2019) and Australia (which uses ACMA enforcement with 90%+ traffic drops), South Africa does not currently block gambling websites.

  • No legislation currently authorises ISP-level website blocking for gambling
  • The Internet Service Providers' Association (ISPA) confirmed in February 2026 that ISP blocking is not legally possible without new legislation
  • The NGB has acknowledged it lacks the resources and expertise to police offshore operators effectively
  • Enforcement relies primarily on exchange control (SARB) and bank payment blocking

How to Protect Yourself

Use Licensed Operators Only

Every bookmaker on this site holds a valid SA provincial licence. Check our licence verification guide to confirm any operator's status.

Look for Licence Details

Licensed operators display their licence number, issuing board, and regulatory information on their website. If you cannot find this information, the operator is likely unlicensed.

Verify with the Board

Contact the relevant provincial gambling board to confirm a licence. Our Legal Hub has contact details for all 9 boards.
