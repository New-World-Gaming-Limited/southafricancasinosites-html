What is a live casino?

A live dealer casino is an online gambling format where professional dealers operate physical casino equipment (roulette wheels, card tables, dice) at a studio or land-based casino, and stream the session to online players who place bets through a website or app interface. Games typically include live roulette, live blackjack, live baccarat, live poker variants and game show products.

Live dealer casinos emerged as a bridge between online slots (automated) and land-based casino environments (in-person). They are widely offered by major international gambling brands.

Is live casino legal in South Africa?

No. Live dealer casino games are categorised as interactive gambling under the National Gambling Act 7 of 2004. The Act's prohibition on interactive gambling applies regardless of whether the dealer is human or automated. No South African provincial licence exists for this product category.

The same prohibition that applies to online slots and online roulette applies to live dealer casino products. Players at offshore live casino sites have no regulatory protection under South African law and no recourse to any South African gambling authority in a dispute.
